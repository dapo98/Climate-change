What You Can Do

Climate change is the most critical and urgent issue facing our world. New York State and City are working to reduce GHG emissions by over 80% by 2050 to help avoid the most drastic effects of climate change. New Yorkers produce more heat trapping gasses than all of Central America and Mexico combined, the State has a tremendous resource in its people, who now have the opportunity to lead the way in finding solutions to climate change and reducing its impact on future generations.

According to Nyc.gov the city is doing the following:

  • Spearheading City initiatives to promote environmental justice and ensure environmental justice is embedded in City decision making Divesting the City’s pension funds from fossil fuels, and investing in climate solutions
  • Taking the fight straight to the fossil fuel industry for their role in creating this climate crisis by suing the five investor-owned fossil fuel companies most responsible for climate change Committing to end the expansion of fossil fuel-related infrastructure in New York City
  • Working with cities around the nation and around the world to scale up climate action efforts and share tools, knowledge, and best practices, to ensure climate change and the interrelated issues of equity and social justice are addressed at a global scale
  • Leading the City’s clean electricity climate advocacy




Individual responsibility

Effort on the part of people has to be done if any progress can be made. Each one of us may have a bigger impact than we realize.

Save energy & Water

Try to reuce the amount of energy used in the home by unplugging devices that are fully charged. Unplug devices and applications that are not in use or rarely used. Use eletronics on low power modes. Try to save water by taking shorter showers and turn off the water while brushing your teeth.

Food Intake

What you eat actually has an impact on climaate change, as the energy used to grow, package and ship the items add up. A large portion is wasted and ends up as landfill, so eating what you buy can reduce this heavy impact. livestock products are among the most resource intensive to produce,cutting down on meat makes a difference too.

Transportation

If it is possible, think about taking public transportation to work, carpool, or even think about a fuel efficient vehicle. Less cars on the road makes the difference. If you are going somewhere like a local grocery store, think about walking or riding a bike. Not only is it good for the environmnet, but also your physical and mental health.

Tell others!

If you can tell your friends, family, schoolmates, and workmates, that is one of the most powerful tools you have. Whether in person or on social meida, they can put in the same steps or do their own research on how important this issue is. They may even tell their frienda about it. It won't cost you anything to tell others!




Although there is not a universal way to stop climate change, each individual has an impact. Even if it may seem like one person cannot make a difference, each one of us is responsible for ourselves. Therefore, it is vital to know what can be done.
